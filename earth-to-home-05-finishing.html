Stage Five of Eight

Finishing

Transformation made the working substance. Finishing turns it into a recognizable thing. Sauce gets seasoned and stabilized. Pulp becomes paper, then board. Steel gets sized, smoothed, and given an edge. After this stage, the product is almost ready — only assembly stands between it and a shelf.

Apple Finishing
○
Pulp → seasoned, pasteurized sauce
Where The same processing plant as cooking and pulping. The pulp leaves the holding tank, gets sweetened and spiced in mixing tanks, then runs through a continuous pasteurizer before the next stage.
Hover either panel to see what's happening at that step.
Output
Shelf-stable applesauce
Sweetened, seasoned, and pasteurized. The recipe is now complete — what you taste from a jar would taste exactly like this if you spooned it straight from the line. All that's left is the container.
Lumber Finishing
▲
Pulp slurry → paper rolls → corrugated board
Where Two facilities, often hundreds of miles apart. The paper machine usually sits inside the kraft mill — the wet pulp goes straight from the digester to the paper machine without ever drying. Finished paper rolls are then trucked or railed to a separate corrugator plant, whose only job is gluing fluted medium between liners.
Hover either panel to see what's happening at that step.
Output
Corrugated board sheets
Continuous board exits the corrugator at ~400–800 feet per minute — roughly 5–9 mph — and is sliced into giant sheets ready for cutting, scoring, and printing in the next stage. A single line can produce millions of square feet per day.
Iron Finishing
◆
Rough forgings → cut, rolled, sharpened blades
Where The scissor factory. Rough forgings arrive in batches and run through a sequence of cutting, rolling, and grinding stations — each one a specialized cell. Most scissor production is concentrated in a few cities globally: Solingen (Germany), Seki (Japan), and Yangjiang (China) dominate.

Output
Sharpened scissor blades
Each blade is now precisely sized, smooth, and sharp. The two halves of a scissor still have to be joined together at the pivot — that's assembly. But the cutting tool itself is now functional. A finished blade off this line can already cut paper, even before it has a partner.
