Stage Six of Eight

Assembly

Finishing made the components. Assembly puts them together into a finished product you could put in someone's hand. Sauce gets sealed in a jar. Board gets folded into a box. Two scissor halves get joined at the pivot. After this stage, the product has its final form — only delivery stands between it and a customer.

Apple Assembly
○
Sauce + jars + lids → finished product
Where The same processing plant as cooking and finishing — the pasteurizer drains directly into the filler. Empty jars arrive on a separate line from a glass plant nearby (or from offshore), get rinsed, and meet the sauce on the filling line.
Hover either panel to see what's happening at that step.
Output
Sealed jars of applesauce
Each jar is now a finished product — pasteurized sauce inside, hermetic seal, label applied. From here it's a sealed unit, indistinguishable from any jar you'd pull off a shelf. Stable for years if unopened.
Lumber Assembly
▲
Sheets → cut, scored, glued, branded boxes
Where A converter — a separate facility from the corrugator, often closer to the brand customer. Giant board sheets arrive on flatbeds; cut, scored, glued, and printed flat-pack boxes leave on flatbeds. A typical converter serves dozens of brand customers within a regional radius.
Hover either panel to see what's happening at that step.
Output
Branded flat-pack box blanks
Stacked on pallets, ready to ship. Each blank is precisely sized, scored where it'll fold, and printed with the customer's branding. They arrive at the buyer flat — the box is only assembled into 3D form at the moment something needs to go inside it.
Iron Assembly
◆
Two blades → joined, handled, packaged scissors
Where The scissor factory. Final assembly is the last cell on the line — riveting, handle attachment, and packaging happen in sequence in the same building where the blades were finished. The product that leaves here is what you'd find in a retail box.
Hover any panel to see what's happening at that step.
Output
Retail-ready scissors
Two pieces of steel, joined at one point, with a handle on each end and a barcode on the package. Functional, branded, sellable. The supply chain that started 15+ years ago at an iron ore pit in Australia now ends at a hangtag on a hardware-store hook.
