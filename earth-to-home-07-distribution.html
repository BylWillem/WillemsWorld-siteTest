Stage Seven of Eight

Distribution

The product is finished. Now it has to get somewhere. Each of our three products travels a fundamentally different distance, through a different number of hops, on a different mix of trucks, ships, and rail. The geography of where things were made determines the geography of how they reach you.

Block 01

Where the products flow

Apple · Domestic loop
○
loading map…

Made in Washington, New York, Michigan, Pennsylvania, Virginia — the apple-growing states. Jars ship out by truck and rail to grocery distribution centers across the country.

Avg. journey 500–1,500 mi · within US
Lumber · Regional radius
▲
loading map…

About ~600 corrugating plants are scattered across the US, each serving customers within a tight regional radius. Empty boxes are mostly air — shipping them far costs more than making them locally.

Avg. journey 50–250 mi · regional only
Scissors · Global inbound
◆
loading map…

Almost all consumer scissors sold in the US are imported from China and Pakistan. They arrive through five major container ports — LA/Long Beach, Seattle, NY/NJ, Savannah, and Houston — then travel inland by rail and truck.

Avg. journey 7,500+ mi · across an ocean
Block 02

How it gets there

Apple · 3 hops
○
Domestic logistics · ~5–18 days total Truck → Rail → Truck → Warehouse
Hover any step to see what happens.
Notable

Sealed jars are shelf-stable — they ride at ambient temperature in regular dry-van trucks. The warehouse is climate-controlled mostly to keep the labels and pallets dry, not the sauce.

Lumber · 1 hop
▲
Single-hop regional · ~1–2 days total Truck → Warehouse
Hover any step to see what happens.
Notable

Flat-pack box blanks ship on pallets directly to the customer's warehouse — usually within a day's drive. A truck of empty boxes hits its volume limit long before its weight limit, so distance is the enemy.

Scissors · 5 hops
◆
Global multimodal · ~25–60 days total Truck → Ship → Rail → Truck → Warehouse
Hover any step to see what happens.
Notable

A pair of scissors travels through 5 different transport modes and crosses an ocean before reaching a US warehouse. The journey takes ~25–60 days from factory to fulfillment center — longer than the entire apple supply chain combined.
