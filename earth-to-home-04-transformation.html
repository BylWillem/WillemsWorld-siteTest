Stage Four of Eight

Transformation

This is the violent stage. Refinement stripped material to its working substance — transformation rearranges that substance into something fundamentally new. Apples become sauce. Wood chips become paper. Pig iron becomes steel. After this, the product is no longer recognizable as what it was.

Apple Transformation
○
Cored flesh → cooked, pulped, smooth sauce
Where The same processing plant as refinement — cooking and pulping happen in the next room over from peeling and coring. The whole flow is continuous: peeled apples fall directly into the cooking kettles.
Hover either panel to see what's happening at that step.
Output
Smooth applesauce base
Cooked, pulped, and ready for finishing. The fruit is now completely unrecognizable as an apple — a homogeneous, lightly sweet purée. From here it's only a matter of seasoning and packaging.
Lumber Transformation
▲
Wood chips → fiber slurry → kraft pulp
Where The pulp mill, continuous flow. Chips that came out of the chipper feed straight into the digester within the same facility. Kraft mills are big enough to be small towns — they run 24/7, with a steady stream of trucks delivering logs at one end and rolls of paper leaving the other.
Hover either panel to see what's happening at that step.
Output
Brown kraft pulp
A wet slurry of pure cellulose fibers, ready to be pressed and dried into paper. The lignin that bound the wood together is now dissolved into black liquor, which the mill burns to power the entire operation.
Iron Transformation
◆
Pig iron → steel → forged blanks
Where The steel mill, which may or may not be next to the blast furnace. Integrated mills do everything in one site (smelting + steelmaking + forging). "Mini-mills" using EAF technology recycle scrap steel anywhere with a power grid. Forging is often a separate facility — sometimes in another country.
Hover either panel to see what's happening at that step.
Output
Forged steel scissor blanks
From ~94% iron / 4% carbon to ~99.5% iron / 0.5% carbon — a small change in composition, a massive change in properties. The metal is now strong and tough rather than brittle. Hammered into rough scissor-shaped blanks, ready for finishing.
